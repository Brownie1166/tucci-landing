The experience

A day with organic winemaker, cheesemaker and shepherd, Michele Cuscusa

Spend a full day at Fattoria Cuscusa — Michele Cuscusa's organic farm and dairy in Gonnostramatza. Michele is a third-generation shepherd, the son of the ancient transumanza tradition that has shaped Sardinian culture for four thousand years. He also makes wine, cooks as an agrichef, and farms with 700 sheep and 300 goats.

A day on his farm is an entry point into an older world: simple food, extraordinary ingredients, a hospitality so natural it feels like memory.

  • TypeFarm visit · Cheesemaking · Wine tasting · Lunch and joint cooking session
  • DurationFull day — departs Cagliari, returns early evening
  • Group sizeSmall group, maximum 8 guests
  • LanguagesItalian and English throughout
  • IncludesDriver, guide, all activities and meals — fully all inclusive
  • Also availableAs part of the 12-day Harvest Intensive tour
  • Upon requestUnderground suckling pig — 12-hour sleepover activity

The day in detail

What to expect at each stage

01
Morning

Arrival at Fattoria Cuscusa

Gonnostramatza · Medio Campidano, South Sardinia

The farm sits in the rolling hills of the Medio Campidano, the bread basket for ancient Rome. Michele will meet you at the gate. Upon arrival you will enjoy the view of the extinct volcano, Monte Arci, the perfumes of the more than 150 indigenous plants on the organic farm. Last but not least, Michele's family come from Sedilo, one of the most important horse back riding towns in Sardinia, explaining why they have some of the most beautiful horses on their farm.

  • Meet Michele and his family — the third generation of shepherds on this land
  • Walk the farm — 700 sheep and 300 goats, organic certified since the beginning
  • See the dairy where the milk becomes cheese, ricotta, gelato and more
  • Understand the transumanza — the ancient shepherding tradition that shaped this island

02
Mid-morning

Cheesemaking with Michele

The dairy at Fattoria Cuscusa

Michele uses techniques passed down through generations — the same methods used on this island for three thousand years. You will work alongside him in the dairy, learning the process from milk to finished cheese. This is not a demonstration. You will make it yourself, with your hands, guided by his.

  • Hands-on cheesemaking — Pecorino, ricotta, fresh varieties
  • Learn traditional Sardinian dairy techniques used for millennia
  • Understand the difference between organic and conventional production
  • Taste at each stage — fresh curd, aged, smoked

03
Midday

Wine tasting from Michele's vines

The vineyard at Fattoria Cuscusa

Michele grows Cannonau, Bovale, Vermentino and Semidano organic grapes with a fraction of added sulphites. I cannot tell you how many people come to the farm and tell me they cannot eat pasta, bread or drink white wine — and leave telling me they have never felt so good after a meal.

  • Visit the vineyard with Michele to understand more about organic wine production
  • Taste Cannonau directly from the farm that grew it
  • Understand why Sardinian wine is unlike anything else in Italy
  • Learn about the different Sardinian wines with a wine expert
  • Taste alongside Michele's own cheeses and other dishes — the pairings as they were always meant to be
Details
DurationApprox. 45 minutes

04
Afternoon

Lunch at Michele's table

The family kitchen · Fattoria Cuscusa

Lunch is cooked together at Michele's family table — in his kitchen, using ingredients from the farm. This is the table that Stanley Tucci sat down to on Tucci in Italy. The same food, the same hands, the same extraordinary hospitality that made the episode what it was.

  • Cook alongside Michele — a meal built from what the farm produces
  • Sardinian fregula and/or Lorighittas (hand made from nearby Morgongiori), farm meat, organic vegetables, fresh cheese
  • Michele's own Cannonau, Vermentino and Semidano on the table throughout
  • At the end of the meal a special treat: most likely the world's oldest and rarest cheeses — Casu Marzu and Calle Crabittu — to be described when we eat them
  • After lunch, an optional hike on the farm or to the nearby Giara di Gesturi to see the indigenous miniature horses

Your host

Michele
Cuscusa

Michele Cuscusa is a shepherd. That is how he introduces himself, and it is the most precise thing you can say about him — he is the son of the transumanza, the ancient shepherding tradition that has shaped Sardinian culture for four thousand years.

He is also a winemaker, an agrichef, and an organic farmer. A man rooted in the oldest traditions of his island who is entirely open to the new. Most guests leave Sardinia thinking about Michele's charismatic and welcoming personality and of course his goat and sheep's milk yogurt based gelato (beelato) and his fregula — Sardinian round pasta — with vegetables.

  • 700 sheep and 300 goats — organic certified
  • Cannonau from his own vines
  • Nearly 100 products from his dairy
  • Gonnostramatza, Medio Campidano
  • As seen on Tucci in Italy · Disney · National Geographic
